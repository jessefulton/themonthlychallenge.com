Challenge Date: 
January, 2009

My goals for the month of January are simple:

  • go on at least one date
  • go to or host an event with >90% strangers
  • sell at least four items
  • give away at least four items
  • find a short-term freelance gig
  • find at least one "missed connection"

The kicker here is that all of this must be done through craigslist.

That's right, I'm gonna meet crazy people from the internet! I'm pretty sure I'll be able to handle the trading of goods and freelancing. Hopefully the date ends hilariously... I'm not so sure how I feel about the "community event" - I'll probably just try to find a local poker game or something.

Oh, and if I actually do get a legitimate response from a "missed connection" post on craigslist - well, that would be ridiculous. They're almost always the most ridiculous things in the world. Man, I'm gonna spam the hell out of that board. I wonder what the chances are of getting a "best of craigslist" post. I think if I make it into that club, then the challenge will be a success.

Also, I've been told by friends with legal knowledge that I can technically post any e-mail reponses I receive from my posts. Granted, I'll probably wait a while to talk about anything I post in the off-chance that one of the few people reading this thinks it'd be funny to find my craigslist ad and reply to me in jest.

Hi, My Name is Craigslist Jesse

Wed, 01/28/2009 - 21:46 | by Jesse

I just got back from my craigslist date, and it was a pretty decent time. We met up at Gin Sushi in Pasadena after work (which by the way, I wouldn't really recommend.) Even though I got lost on the way there, I still managed to make it there before my date and was waiting in the restaurant for a little while before she got there.

After the second set of patrons to come through the door failed to be my date, I began getting really nervous. What if I don't recognize her? Do I shake her hand when she comes in or do we hug? But I'm a terrible hugger! What if she stands me up? What if I get stood up by a craigslist date! I'm a loser! Fortunately, she showed up before I had a nervous breakdown and was surprisingly laid back and cool.
