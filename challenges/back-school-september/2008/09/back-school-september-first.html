Happy end of Labor Day Weekend! After a weekend full of scavenger hunting, sleeping, eating, and beaching, it's time to rebuild all of those brain cells lost during Alcoholic August. I have to go back to school tomorrow to try to get into an illustration class, but in addition to the art classes I'm planning to take, there is a short list of other things I'd like to learn and/or re-learn:

  • Japanese
  • Spanish
  • Photoshop
  • Flash
  • php
  • UML
  • Design Patterns
  • Guitar/Musical scales and basic theory
  • Basic Motorcycle Repair

So every day, I will try to learn something new. And I don't mean just like reading a blog post about a neat-o Photoshop trick, or learn how to say "your toupee is over there" in Spanish - I'm talking larnin' learning. Like full lessons, just like back in school (when I actually attended class.)

I'm also going to try to finish reading a book I just started today - Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance (which has nothing to do with the last item on my list up above there.) If you know me, then you know that I normally only read books at airports. That is slowly beginning to change, and I'm pretty sure I can get through a 500+ page book in a month. Other goals have not yet been set...do I need to find online quizzes and tests? Or can I just be comfortable knowing that I learned something new?

I think I'll count any sort of in-depth, on the job training or presentations as well as museums (and zoos?) Maybe I can finally get in to one of those improv classes too - or the advanced motorcycle riding course. That's educational.
