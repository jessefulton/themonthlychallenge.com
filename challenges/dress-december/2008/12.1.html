Collared Shirts Are Not Your Friend

Mon, 12/15/2008 - 21:12 | by Jesse

I went skiing this weekend, and somebody commented that you get used to dressing up at work. "After a few weeks, dressing up is the norm and is not uncomfortable." It may not be terribly uncomfortable, but it's definitely less comfortable than jeans and a T-shirt. That's why people don't get dressed up when they're relaxing on the weekends, or when they're doing yard work, or when they're ready for a nice, comfy nap.

Like lipstick on a pig - a really, really good looking pig

Wed, 12/10/2008 - 12:48 | by Jesse

So far, only one person at work has actively commented on my new attire. Personally, I feel a bit more uncomfortable and probably less productive. I interviewed someone last week, and I'm hoping that he thought I was more professional (rather than more lame) for wearing a sport coat and a T-shirt. I'm conducting another interview tomorrow, and am trying to debate how I can dress to impress the candidate. I don't want him to think we're a laid back bunch of slackers at work. Or do I...?
